DE800 Key Specifications

  • Power Rating: 120W RMS
  • Magnet Type: Neodymium (Imported quality) — lighter and more powerful than ferrite
  • Type: HF (High Frequency) Compression Driver / Tweeter
  • Application: DJ speaker cabinets, PA tops, line array elements
  • Frequency Range: 1kHz–20kHz (ideal crossover point: 2kHz–3kHz)

Why Neodymium? Ferrite vs Neodymium Explained

Traditional speaker drivers use ferrite (ceramic) magnets — heavy, affordable, and reliable. The DE800 uses a neodymium magnet, which is significantly more powerful for its size and weight. This means:

  • Higher sensitivity — more output per watt (typically 2–3dB more efficient)
  • Lighter cabinet weight — important for touring and regular transport in India
  • Better high-frequency extension — neodymium drivers typically reach higher frequencies more cleanly
  • Premium feel and build quality — the DE800's imported neodymium magnet gives it a professional grade appearance and performance

How to Use the DE800 in Your Speaker Cabinet

Crossover Point

The DE800 should be crossed over between 2kHz and 3kHz. Set your crossover network's high-pass filter (HPF) to 2.5kHz for the DE800 output. Below this frequency, the HF driver can be damaged by excessive power. Use a quality crossover network plate — available at Punishka Trading — to protect the driver.

Mounting

The DE800 uses standard horn throat threading. It pairs with most 1-inch and 1.5-inch horn throats. Screw it firmly into the horn, ensuring no air gaps at the mounting point — gaps cause distortion and reduced output.

Power Matching

The DE800 is rated at 120W RMS. Pair it with an amplifier channel delivering 80–120W at the crossover's HF output. Using our PU8000 amplifier in tri-amp mode, each DE800 receives clean, optimally powered high-frequency signal for maximum clarity.

What DJ Events Sound Better with the DE800?

Any event that demands vocal clarity and high-frequency detail benefits from the DE800:

  • Wedding and sangeet events — anchors and singers sound crystal clear
  • Corporate conferences — speech intelligibility is dramatically improved
  • DJ sets — hi-hats, synths, and high-frequency music details cut through the mix
  • Live instrument performances — guitars, keyboards, and percussion sound natural and detailed
