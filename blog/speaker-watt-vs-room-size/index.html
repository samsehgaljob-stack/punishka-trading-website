Speaker Watt vs Room Size — Complete Calculator

📅 22 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

Choosing the right speaker wattage for your room size is the single biggest decision that separates crisp, full sound from distorted mush or weak background noise. At our Rohini showroom, we get this question daily from wedding DJs, event planners, and café owners. This guide gives you a clear formula, real examples, and the exact speakers to buy for each venue size in India.

The Simple Watt-Per-Person Rule

Here's a field-tested rule used by professional sound engineers in India:

  • Background music (café, restaurant): 2–4 watts RMS per person
  • Live speech or small event: 5–10 watts RMS per person
  • DJ party or wedding: 15–20 watts RMS per person
  • Loud concert or club: 25–35 watts RMS per person

Important: These are RMS (continuous) watts, not peak or PMPO numbers. A speaker advertised as "2000W PMPO" usually has only 200–300W RMS.

Room Size to Wattage — Quick Table

  • Small room (up to 500 sq ft, 25 people): 250–400W total RMS. One pair of 8" or 10" speakers.
  • Medium hall (500–1500 sq ft, 50–100 people): 600–1200W total RMS. Pair of 12" or 15" speakers.
  • Banquet hall (1500–3500 sq ft, 150–300 people): 1500–3500W total RMS. 2× 15" tops + 2× 18" subwoofers.
  • Wedding lawn (3500–8000 sq ft, 300–600 people): 4000–8000W total RMS. Line array + dual 18" subs + DJ monitor.
  • Open ground (8000+ sq ft, 600+ people): 10,000W+ total RMS. Professional line-array system.

Matching Amplifier to Speaker

Once you know your speaker wattage requirement, your amplifier should be 1.5× to 2× the total speaker RMS. Under-powering causes clipping (which destroys speakers faster than over-powering). For a pair of 400W RMS speakers, use an amp delivering around 600–800W per channel — the PU8000 fits this range perfectly.

Why Indian Venues Need More Watts

Three factors make Indian events demand more wattage than Western equivalents:

  • Open-air weddings — sound dissipates rapidly in open venues; you need 40% more power than an equivalent indoor setup.
  • High ambient noise — traffic, cheering, and background chatter add 15–20 dB to the noise floor.
  • Bass-heavy music — Bollywood and punjabi DJ sets demand serious subwoofer power; budget at least 30% of your total wattage for subs.
