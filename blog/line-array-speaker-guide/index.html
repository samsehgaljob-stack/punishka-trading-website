Line Array Speaker System — Complete Guide for India

If you've attended a large concert, wedding reception, or conference in Delhi NCR, you've likely seen tall vertical columns of speakers hanging from the ceiling or mounted on stands. These are line array speaker systems — the gold standard for large-venue sound reinforcement. This guide explains everything you need to know about line arrays for Indian events.

What Is a Line Array?

A line array is a series of speaker cabinets arranged vertically in a column. Each cabinet contains multiple drivers — typically tweeters and mid-range drivers — aligned to work together as a single coherent sound source. When stacked vertically, they produce a cylindrical wave pattern that travels farther and more evenly than traditional point-source speakers.

Line Array vs Traditional PA Speakers

  • Coverage: Line arrays deliver consistent volume from front to back of a large venue. Traditional PA speakers lose volume quickly with distance.
  • Feedback rejection: Line arrays have a tight horizontal dispersion pattern, reducing feedback from microphones on stage.
  • Sound clarity: In large reverberant halls (like Delhi banquet halls and marriage gardens), line arrays provide clearer, more intelligible sound.
  • Cost: Line arrays are more expensive than traditional PA setups but deliver professional results in large venues.

When Should You Use a Line Array?

Line arrays are ideal when:

  • Venue capacity exceeds 300–400 people
  • The venue is a long hall, open garden, or auditorium
  • Sound needs to reach 30–50 meters without losing clarity
  • You're doing a corporate event, concert, or large shaadi in Delhi NCR

For smaller events under 200 people, traditional PA speakers paired with a quality subwoofer system are more cost-effective.

Active vs Passive Line Arrays

Active line arrays have built-in amplifiers in each cabinet — plug and play, easy to transport. Passive line arrays require external amplifiers but give you more control and flexibility. Most professional event companies in Delhi NCR use passive line arrays with PU8000 or PU7800 class amplifiers for maximum power and customization.

Line Array Setup Tips for Indian Events

  • Always fly (hang) line arrays when possible — elevated placement dramatically improves coverage
  • Aim the bottom of the array toward the last row of the audience
  • Pair with 18-inch subwoofers on the ground for deep bass reinforcement
  • Use a digital mixer with DSP for precise delay and EQ alignment
