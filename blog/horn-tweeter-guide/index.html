Horn Tweeter in PA Speaker — How It Works & Why It Matters

📅 May 01, 2026
Englishहिंदी

What Is a Horn Tweeter?

A horn tweeter is a high-frequency driver that uses a horn-shaped acoustic waveguide to amplify and direct sound waves. Unlike a conventional dome tweeter, a horn tweeter achieves very high efficiency — often 100–110 dB/W/m — by using the horn's geometry to transform the small movement of the diaphragm into a large volume of air movement. This makes horn tweeters the preferred choice for professional PA speakers and DJ systems across India, where high output levels are needed to fill large venues without consuming excessive amplifier power.

How Does a Horn Tweeter Work?

Inside a horn tweeter, a compression driver contains a diaphragm made of aluminium, titanium, or polyimide. This diaphragm vibrates at high frequencies (typically 1 kHz and above). The horn throat concentrates this vibration, then the horn bell gradually expands it, coupling the driver efficiently to the air. The result: exceptional loudness, long throw distance, and a controlled dispersion pattern. Types of Horn Tweeters

1. Exponential Horn

The most common type. The horn flare follows an exponential curve, providing wide frequency response and smooth rolloff. Best for general PA use at weddings, events, and DJ setups.

2. Constant Directivity (CD) Horn

Designed to maintain consistent dispersion across frequencies. Especially useful in large venues where even sound coverage is critical — corporate events, auditoriums, and outdoor stages.

3. Tractrix Horn

Based on a mathematical tractrix curve, this horn minimises internal reflections, giving the cleanest, most phase-coherent sound. Found in high-end PA speakers.

Why Horn Tweeters Are Essential for DJ PA Systems

In a DJ PA system, the tweeter must handle sustained high SPL (Sound Pressure Level) during long events. Dome tweeters overheat and blow at high power. Horn tweeters dissipate heat efficiently through the compression driver's rear chamber. Crossover Point — Getting It Right

Horn tweeters should only reproduce frequencies above their rated crossover point — typically 1.5 kHz to 2.5 kHz. Feeding low frequencies into a tweeter is the fastest way to blow it. Always use a properly rated crossover network — passive or active — to protect the tweeter. Punishka Trading stocks a full range of crossover network plates for 2-way and 3-way speaker cabinets.

Installation Tips

Mount the horn tweeter at ear level or above for best coverage. Angle it slightly downward for seated audiences. Ensure the horn throat is clear of any obstructions. Use the correct gauge wire (minimum 16 AWG) between the crossover and the tweeter terminals. Check polarity — reverse polarity causes phase cancellation that destroys bass clarity.
