DJ Speaker Cabinet Types — Which One Do You Need?

📅 22 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

A speaker cabinet isn't just a wooden box — its shape, internal bracing, port design, and woofer placement completely change how your sound behaves. Walk into any Delhi DJ market and you'll see dozens of cabinet styles, each promising more bass or better projection. This guide cuts through the marketing and explains the DJ speaker cabinet types that actually matter in 2025.

1. Bass Reflex (Ported) Cabinets

The most common cabinet in Indian DJ setups. A bass reflex cabinet has a tuned port that extends low-frequency response by using the rear wave of the woofer. Ideal for general PA use, weddings, and small-to-mid events.

  • Typical sizes: 12", 15", 18"
  • Strengths: Good bass extension, lightweight, affordable
  • Best for: Weddings, receptions, general DJ
  • Delhi price: ₹8,000 – ₹25,000 empty cabinet

2. Horn-Loaded Cabinets

Horn-loaded cabinets use a folded horn design to amplify sound mechanically. This gives you huge output with less amplifier power — perfect for large outdoor events.

  • Typical sizes: 15", 18", dual 18"
  • Strengths: Extremely loud, high efficiency, projects sound far
  • Best for: Wedding lawns, outdoor concerts, DJ competitions
  • Delhi price: ₹22,000 – ₹65,000 empty cabinet

3. Line-Array Cabinets

The premium choice for pro events. Line-array cabinets are small, trapezoidal boxes hung vertically. They create a narrow, long-throwing sound field perfect for covering 500+ people evenly.

  • Typical sizes: Single 8", single 10", dual 10"
  • Strengths: Even coverage, long throw, pro aesthetics
  • Best for: Concerts, corporate events, premium weddings
  • Delhi price: ₹35,000 – ₹1,20,000 per box

4. Subwoofer Cabinets

Dedicated low-frequency cabinets for the 30Hz–120Hz range. Without a proper subwoofer cabinet, Bollywood and punjabi music loses its punch.

  • Typical sizes: Single 18", dual 18", 21"
  • Types: Bass reflex, front-loaded horn, folded horn
  • Best for: Any DJ setup serious about bass
  • Delhi price: ₹15,000 – ₹85,000 empty cabinet

5. Two-Way vs Three-Way Cabinets

Two-way cabinets have a woofer + tweeter (most common). Three-way cabinets add a dedicated midrange driver for cleaner vocals — essential for live-band + DJ setups.

Which Cabinet Type Should You Buy?

Starter DJ: Pair of 15" bass reflex tops + single 18" subwoofer. Budget ₹55,000.

Wedding DJ: 2× 15" horn-loaded tops + 2× dual-18" subs. Budget ₹1,50,000+.

Professional / Large Event: Line-array 6-box system + 2× subwoofer stack. Budget ₹4,00,000+.
