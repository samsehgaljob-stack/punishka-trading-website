UHF vs VHF Wireless Microphones

VHF (Very High Frequency) Microphones

VHF mics operate in the 100–300 MHz band. They are cheaper (₹800–₹2,500 per set) and widely used for basic events. However, VHF is prone to interference from mobile phones, TV signals, and other electronic devices — a serious problem in crowded Indian venues and outdoor events. Not recommended for professional use.

UHF (Ultra High Frequency) Microphones

UHF mics operate in the 400–900 MHz band and are the professional standard worldwide. They offer:

  • Much lower interference even in crowded RF environments
  • Longer range — typically 50–100 metres line of sight
  • Better audio quality and dynamic range
  • Frequency-switchable models available to avoid local interference

Quality UHF wireless mic sets in India cost ₹3,500–₹15,000+ depending on brand and features. This is the minimum recommended standard for professional Indian event companies.

Single vs Dual Wireless Mic Systems

A dual wireless system includes two handheld transmitters (or one handheld + one lapel/headset) and a single receiver unit. This is the standard for most Indian event and DJ setups where an anchor and a co-host or performer both need mics simultaneously.

Key advantage of dual systems: both channels share one receiver unit and one audio output, reducing rack space and cabling. Price range in Delhi: ₹5,000–₹20,000 for professional dual UHF systems.

What to Look For in a Wireless Mic

  • Frequency band: Always choose UHF over VHF for any professional use
  • Battery life: Should be minimum 6–8 hours for Indian events
  • Squelch sensitivity: Auto-mutes receiver when transmitter is off — avoids noise bursts
  • XLR balanced output: Essential for long cable runs to your mixer
  • Diversity reception: Two antennas in receiver reduce dropout risk significantly
  • Capsule quality: Cardioid polar pattern recommended for stage/event use
