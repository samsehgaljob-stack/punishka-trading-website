Why Every DJ Needs a Good Subwoofer

A subwoofer handles the low-frequency content (typically 20Hz–120Hz) that gives music its power, energy, and physical impact. Without a dedicated subwoofer, your DJ system — no matter how good your main speakers are — will lack the deep bass thump that gets people dancing. For Indian events like weddings, jagrans, sangeets, and club nights, a powerful subwoofer is non-negotiable.

18-Inch vs 15-Inch Subwoofer — Which is Better?

18-Inch Subwoofer

The 18-inch subwoofer is the professional standard for most Indian DJ and event rigs. It moves more air, goes deeper (down to 30–40Hz), and can fill large outdoor spaces. An 18-inch driver in a ported bass reflex cabinet paired with a 1000–2000W amplifier will shake the ground at a 500-person wedding. Delhi wholesale price per cabinet: ₹5,000–₹18,000 depending on driver spec.

15-Inch Subwoofer

The 15-inch sub is more compact and lighter — easier to transport. Better suited for indoor events, banquet halls, and smaller venues where you need controlled bass without overwhelming the room. Price: ₹3,500–₹10,000.

Key Subwoofer Specifications Explained

  • RMS Power Handling: The continuous power the subwoofer can handle. Match to your amplifier's RMS output (within ±50%)
  • Sensitivity (dB SPL): A higher sensitivity means more volume for the same power. Look for 95dB+ for professional use.
  • Frequency Response: Lower limit (e.g., 35Hz) tells you how deep the bass goes. Outdoor events benefit from flatter, lower extension.
  • Voice Coil Diameter: Larger voice coils (3" or 4") handle more power and heat better than smaller ones
  • Xmax (excursion): How far the cone moves. More Xmax = more bass at lower frequencies without distortion.

Best Subwoofer Setup for Indian Events

For professional outdoor events in Delhi/NCR, the recommended setup is:

  • Medium events (200–400 guests): 2× 18-inch subwoofer cabinets powered by 1× 2-channel amplifier in bridge mode (2000–3000W per channel)
  • Large outdoor events (500+ guests): 4× 18-inch subs in a cardioid or standard stack arrangement, powered by 2× 4-channel amplifiers
