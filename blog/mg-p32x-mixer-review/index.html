MG-P32X — The Professional 32-Channel Mixer for Large Events

The MG-P32X 32-Channel Professional Mixer is the top-of-the-line mixing console from Punishka Trading, designed for large-scale live events, concerts, and multi-performer productions in India. With 32 input channels, comprehensive EQ on every channel, multiple aux sends for monitor mixes, and a robust build quality, the MG-P32X is the choice of serious sound engineers and professional DJ companies across Delhi-NCR.

MG-P32X Specifications

  • Channels: 32 — 24 mono XLR/TRS combo + 8 stereo line channels
  • EQ: 4-band semi-parametric EQ on mono channels
  • Aux Sends: 6 aux sends (4 pre-fader for monitors, 2 post-fader for effects)
  • Groups: 4 subgroups for zone control
  • Outputs: Main XLR L/R, mono sub out, 4 group outs, 6 aux outs
  • Inserts: Insert points on every mono channel for outboard processing
  • Phantom Power: +48V in groups of 8
  • Meters: 12-segment LED meters on main and groups
  • USB/Recording: 2-track USB recording and playback

Why 32 Channels for Indian Events?

At large Indian functions — a grand wedding with a live band, a concert with multiple artists, a college fest — inputs quickly add up: 6–8 vocal mics, drum kit (6–8 mics), guitar, bass, keyboard, DJ sources, playback, and stage monitors. A 32-channel mixer like the MG-P32X handles it all without compromise, giving the sound engineer full control over every source on stage.

Semi-Parametric EQ — Precise Sound Shaping

The 4-band semi-parametric EQ on every channel gives you precise control over the frequency spectrum. Unlike basic 3-band shelving EQ on budget mixers, the MG-P32X mid-bands are sweepable — you can identify and cut problem frequencies precisely. This is critical for live event sound in reverberant Indian wedding venues.

Setting Up the MG-P32X for a Large Event

  1. Connect all stage sources via a 16 or 24-channel audio snake to the MG-P32X inputs
  2. Assign monitor mixes to aux sends 1–4 (pre-fader)
  3. Route main PA to main XLR output into your amplifier rack
  4. Use subgroups to control drums, vocals, and music separately
  5. Connect aux send 5–6 to outboard reverb unit for vocal effects
