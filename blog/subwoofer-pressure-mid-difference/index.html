Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — What's the Difference?

📅 20 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

In Indian DJ and tent-house circles, two terms cause the most confusion: subwoofer and pressure mid. Some buyers think they do the same job. They do not. Mixing them up wastes money, ruins sound quality, and can even damage equipment. At Punishka Trading, Rohini, New Delhi, we explain the difference clearly every day — here is the complete answer.

What is a Subwoofer?

A subwoofer is a speaker cabinet dedicated to low-frequency bass, typically 30–120 Hz. It uses an 18-inch or 21-inch driver in a large ported or folded-horn enclosure. A sub gives you the chest-punch kick of a dhol, the rumble of EDM drops, and the deep foundation that makes a sangeet night feel alive.

In Indian weddings, a subwoofer is essential any time you have more than 150 guests or play bhangra, hip-hop, EDM or Bollywood club tracks.

What is a Pressure Mid?

A "pressure mid" (sometimes called a mid-bass cabinet or "dhol" cabinet in Delhi slang) is a separate cabinet designed for low-mid frequencies, roughly 120–500 Hz. It uses a 12-inch or 15-inch driver in a specialized enclosure that "pressurizes" the air to deliver punchy vocals, dhol claps and guitar body. This fills the gap between sub and top speakers.

Pressure mids are a uniquely Indian concept popular in Delhi-Punjab-Haryana wedding circuits. They give the signature "thick" vocal sound that Indian audiences love on sangeet stage shows.

Key Frequency Differences

  • Subwoofer: 30–120 Hz — pure bass, felt more than heard, low-pass at ~100 Hz in KMC8 DSP
  • Pressure Mid: 120–500 Hz — body of vocals, kick drum snap, guitar mids, band-passed in DSP
  • Top Speaker: 500 Hz–20 kHz — clarity, vocals, cymbals, high-pass at 500 Hz

Do You Need Both?

For setups under 300 guests, a 2-way system (top + sub) with a good PU8000 amp and KMC8 DSP is enough. For 300+ guest weddings, sangeets with live band, or outdoor events where you want that loaded Delhi DJ sound, a 3-way system with pressure mid + sub + top delivers noticeably better mid-range clarity and vocal presence.

Amplifier Needs

Subwoofers need big bridged power — one PU8000 channel bridged delivers 1,600W+ for a double 18-inch sub. Pressure mids need medium power — 600–1,000W per cabinet. Top speakers need clean mid power — 400–800W. Route all three through an 8-in/8-out DSP like the KMC8 for precise crossover, delay and limiter control.
