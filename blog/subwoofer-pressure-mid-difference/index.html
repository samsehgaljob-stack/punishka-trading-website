Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — What's the Difference?

📅 20 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

If you've walked into any pro audio shop in Delhi and asked for "bass", the shopkeeper has probably shown you two very different cabinets — one called a subwoofer and one called a pressure mid. These look similar from the outside but do fundamentally different jobs. Understanding the difference will save you tens of thousands of rupees on a wrong purchase.

Quick Answer

A subwoofer reproduces the lowest frequencies — typically 20 Hz to 100 Hz — the physical "thump" you feel in your chest during a bass drop. A pressure mid (also called mid-bass driver or punch cabinet) reproduces the mid-bass and vocal-punch range — 150 Hz to 2 kHz — delivering the clarity of drums, dhol strikes, and male vocals. You need both in a complete DJ rig.

Frequency Range — The Core Difference

  • Subwoofer: 20–100 Hz. Non-directional. You feel it more than you hear it.
  • Pressure Mid: 150 Hz – 2 kHz. Highly directional. Delivers kick-drum attack and vocal presence.

The frequency gap between 100 Hz and 150 Hz is handled by either the subwoofer's top rolloff, the pressure mid's bottom rolloff, or — better — a well-tuned crossover.

Driver Design

Subwoofers use large drivers (15", 18", 21") with long-throw voice coils, heavy magnets, and stiff cones to move huge volumes of air at low frequencies. They prioritize displacement over speed.

Pressure mids use medium drivers (10", 12", 15") with shorter-throw but faster voice coils. They prioritize transient response — the ability to start and stop the cone quickly, which gives kick-drum its punch.

Cabinet Design

Subwoofer cabinets are large, heavily braced, and usually bass-reflex or bandpass tuned to 35–45 Hz. Pressure mid cabinets are smaller, often horn-loaded, and tuned higher (120–200 Hz).

Why Indian DJ Setups Need Both

Bollywood and Punjabi music have two distinct low-end elements: the deep sub-bass synth (say, the dropping "boom" in a club remix) and the percussive dhol/tabla/kick (the "ta-thak"). A subwoofer handles the first, a pressure mid handles the second. A good wedding setup uses a 4-way system: subwoofer + pressure mid + top (mids/highs) + tweeter — all driven from a flagship amp like the PU8000.

Common Wiring Mistake

Many first-time DJ buyers wire a pressure mid to the subwoofer channel of their amp — and wonder why the bass sounds thin. Pressure mids are NOT subwoofers. They need their own amp channel and their own crossover band (typically 150 Hz – 1 kHz). See our amplifier guide and mixer guide for correct setup diagrams.

Frequency Range — मुख्य फर्क

  • Subwoofer: 20–100 Hz. Non-directional. सुनाई कम, महसूस ज़्यादा होता है.
  • Pressure Mid: 150 Hz – 2 kHz. Highly directional. Kick-drum attack और vocal presence.

Driver Design

Subwoofer में बड़े driver (15", 18", 21") long-throw voice coil, heavy magnet, stiff cone के साथ होते हैं — ज़्यादा हवा move करते हैं. Pressure mid में medium driver (10", 12", 15") short-throw पर fast voice coil — transient response बेहतर. यानि cone जल्दी start-stop होता है, इसलिए punch मिलता है.
