Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — What's the Difference?

In Indian DJ equipment circles, one question that comes up repeatedly is: subwoofer vs pressure mid — what exactly is the difference and which one does your setup need? Many beginners confuse these two types of speakers, leading to incorrect setups and poor sound. This guide clarifies both speaker types and helps you understand how to use them correctly.

What Is a Subwoofer?

A subwoofer is a loudspeaker specifically designed to reproduce the lowest audible frequencies — typically 20Hz to 120Hz. It handles the deep bass kicks, bass guitar notes, and sub-bass elements that create the physical "feel" of music. A good 18-inch subwoofer can produce bass you don't just hear but physically feel in your chest. Subwoofers are always used in conjunction with mid-range or full-range speakers — they cannot reproduce the entire frequency spectrum alone.

In Indian events: Subwoofers are essential for weddings, DJ nights, and concerts where bass-heavy music like Bollywood, EDM, and bhangra is played. Wholesale prices for quality 18-inch subwoofer drivers in Rohini range from ₹2,200 to ₹8,000.

What Is a Pressure Mid Speaker?

A pressure mid (also called a pressure driver or compression mid) is a specialized speaker driver that produces extremely high sound pressure levels in the mid-frequency range (approximately 200Hz–5kHz). Unlike conventional cone drivers, pressure mids use a compression driver mechanism that creates very high efficiency — often 95–105 dB/W/m compared to a regular speaker's 85–90 dB/W/m. This means a pressure mid can produce dramatically louder sound with the same amplifier power.

Pressure mids are the secret weapon of Indian outdoor sound systems. They give that characteristic "loud and punchy" mid-range that cuts through crowd noise at melas, outdoor weddings, and religious functions. A pressure mid cabinet typically houses a 6-inch or 8-inch compression driver on a horn flare.

Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — Key Differences

  • Frequency range: Subwoofer handles 20–120Hz; Pressure mid handles 200Hz–5kHz
  • Use case: Subwoofer for bass; Pressure mid for powerful mid-range projection
  • Efficiency: Pressure mids are 8–12 dB more efficient than standard cone drivers
  • Applications: Subwoofer = indoor/outdoor bass; Pressure mid = outdoor long-throw projection
  • Price: 18-inch sub drivers ₹2,200–₹8,000; Pressure mid units ₹1,800–₹6,500 in Rohini
