Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — What's the Difference?

📅 22 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

If you've shopped for DJ speakers in India, you've probably heard sellers talk about "subwoofer" and "pressure mid" almost interchangeably — but they're not the same thing. Mixing these up is one of the most common mistakes new DJs make, and it leads to muddy, bass-heavy, unclear sound. Let's clear this up once and for all.

What Is a Subwoofer?

A subwoofer is a speaker designed exclusively for low frequencies — typically 30 Hz to 120 Hz. It reproduces kick drums, 808 bass lines, sub-bass drops in EDM, and the deep punch you feel in your chest during a wedding DJ set.

  • Typical woofer size: 18" (most common in India), 15", 21"
  • Frequency range: 30–120 Hz
  • Role: Felt, not heard as clearly — adds physical energy to the sound
  • Delhi price: ₹18,000 – ₹85,000 for a loaded subwoofer cabinet

What Is a Pressure Mid Speaker?

A pressure mid speaker (sometimes called "mid-bass" or "upper bass") is designed for the 100 Hz to 500 Hz range — the body of kick drums, the fullness of vocals, and the warmth of Bollywood instruments like dhol and tabla.

  • Typical woofer size: 10", 12", 15"
  • Frequency range: 100–500 Hz (some extend to 800 Hz)
  • Role: The "push" and "punch" you clearly hear
  • Delhi price: ₹12,000 – ₹38,000 per cabinet

Side-by-Side Comparison

  • Frequency: Sub = 30–120 Hz. Mid = 100–500 Hz.
  • What you hear: Sub = chest punch, rumble. Mid = clarity, body.
  • Typical size: Sub = 18" most common. Mid = 12–15".
  • Role in mix: Sub = foundation. Mid = presence.
  • Needed for: Sub = any wedding, party. Mid = live vocals, large outdoor, 3-way systems.

Do You Need Both?

In a 2-way setup (top + subwoofer), the top speaker handles everything from ~100 Hz up, including the midrange. You don't need a separate pressure mid.

In a 3-way setup (top + pressure mid + sub), each driver handles a dedicated band, giving cleaner separation. This is common at large outdoor events and wedding lawns where clarity matters.

Common Mistake: Treating a Pressure Mid as a Subwoofer

We see this weekly — a DJ buys a 15" "pressure mid" cabinet, thinking it will give them deep bass. Then at the event, the kick drum feels weak and they blame the amplifier. The real problem? A 15" pressure mid simply can't go below ~80 Hz. You need a proper 18" subwoofer for real low-end.

Subwoofer vs Pressure Mid — क्या फर्क है?

Subwoofer और Pressure Mid में क्या फर्क है?

India में DJ speaker बेचने वाले अक्सर subwoofer और pressure mid को एक जैसा बता देते हैं — लेकिन दोनों अलग काम के लिए होते हैं। गलत चुनाव से sound muddy आती है। आइए समझते हैं असली फर्क।

Subwoofer क्या है?

Subwoofer सिर्फ low frequency (30Hz-120Hz) के लिए बना speaker है। Kick drum, 808 bass, EDM sub-bass, और शादी में सीने में महसूस होने वाला punch यही देता है।

  • Woofer Size: 18" सबसे common, 15", 21"
  • Frequency: 30-120 Hz
  • Role: सुनाई कम, महसूस ज्यादा
  • Delhi Price: ₹18,000 – ₹85,000 loaded cabinet

Pressure Mid क्या है?

Pressure mid (या mid-bass) 100-500Hz के लिए बना है। Vocals की fullness, dhol, tabla, और kick drum का body यही देता है।

  • Size: 10", 12", 15"
  • Frequency: 100-500 Hz
  • Role: "Push" और "punch" जो आप साफ सुनते हैं
  • Price: ₹12,000 – ₹38,000

सीधी तुलना

  • Frequency: Sub = 30-120Hz। Mid = 100-500Hz।
  • क्या सुनते हैं: Sub = chest punch। Mid = clarity।
  • Size: Sub = 18"। Mid = 12-15"।
  • Role: Sub = foundation। Mid = presence।

क्या दोनों चाहिए?

2-way setup (top + sub) में top speaker ~100Hz से ऊपर सब handle करता है। अलग pressure mid की जरूरत नहीं।

3-way setup (top + mid + sub) में हर driver अपना band handle करता है। Clean separation मिलती है। बड़े outdoor events के लिए अच्छा।
