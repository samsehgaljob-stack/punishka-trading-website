Complete Sound System Setup for Indian Wedding — Full Guide

Setting up a professional sound system for an Indian wedding requires careful planning of equipment selection, speaker placement, signal routing, and cabling. Whether you're a DJ doing your first large wedding or an experienced professional expanding your rig, this guide covers the complete process for Delhi NCR weddings in 2025.

Step 1 — Assess the Venue

Before selecting equipment, visit the venue or get its dimensions:

  • Guest count: Determines total wattage needed (see our Speaker Watt vs Room Size guide)
  • Indoor or outdoor: Indoor requires less power; outdoor needs 2–3× more
  • Ceiling height: High ceilings need more power and careful speaker aiming
  • Power supply: Confirm generator capacity — large systems need 15–25A circuits
  • Stage location: Where is the performance area? This determines speaker placement angles

Step 2 — Select Your Equipment

For a Medium Wedding (200–400 guests) in Delhi NCR

  • 4× 15-inch passive PA speakers (600W RMS each)
  • 2× 18-inch subwoofer cabinets (1000W RMS each)
  • 1× PU7800 amplifier for PA tops (stereo)
  • 1× PU7800 amplifier for subs (bridged or stereo)
  • 1× KMC8 or 16-channel DSP mixer
  • 1× Dual UHF wireless mic system
  • 1× Active crossover (set to 80Hz sub/top split)
  • Speaker cables (Speakon), XLR cables, power distribution

Step 3 — Speaker Placement

Correct speaker placement is as important as equipment quality:

  • PA tops: Place on stands at 2–2.5 metres height, angled down 10–15° toward the audience centre. Position at the front corners of the audience area, not behind the stage.
  • Subwoofers: Place on the ground at the front of the stage. Stacking two subs vertically (one on top of the other) increases bass output and directivity.
  • Delay speakers: For very long venues, add delay speakers midway through the audience area — timed 20–30ms behind the main speakers to prevent echo.

Step 4 — Signal Flow and Routing

The signal chain for a complete wedding setup:

  1. DJ laptop / phone → Mixer input channels (via XLR or RCA)
  2. Wireless mic receivers → Mixer mic input channels
  3. Mixer main output (XLR) → Active crossover input
  4. Crossover high output → PU7800 (Amp 1) → PA tops
  5. Crossover low output → PU7800 (Amp 2) → Subwoofers
  6. Mixer monitor output → Stage monitor speakers (if needed)

Step 5 — Soundcheck Checklist

  • Check each wireless mic for range and dropout-free operation across the full venue
  • Play test music at event volume — check for feedback, distortion, or uneven coverage
  • Walk the audience area while music plays — verify volume is even front-to-back
  • Check all cable connections are secure (Speakon locked, XLR locked)
  • Confirm crossover is set correctly — no bass from PA tops, no midrange from subs
  • Set amplifier input sensitivity so mixer is at 0dB when amps are at 80% volume

Essential Spare Equipment

Always carry to every wedding event: spare XLR cables, spare Speakon cables, extra AA batteries for wireless mics, a spare wireless mic unit, cable ties and gaffer tape, and a torch for dark backstage areas.
