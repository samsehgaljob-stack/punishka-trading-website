Complete Sound System Setup for Indian Wedding

📅 21 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

Planning the sound system setup for an Indian wedding is not just about loud speakers — it's about covering every function (haldi, mehendi, sangeet, baraat, pheras, reception) with clear vocals, full-bodied music, and zero feedback. This guide walks you through exactly what equipment you need, wattage recommendations, and approximate Delhi/NCR wholesale prices in 2025–2026.

Understanding the Events — Why Each Needs Different Sound

A mehendi or haldi needs softer, intimate audio with a few mics for songs. A sangeet night needs full DJ-grade bass and mids for dance performances. A baraat requires portable but powerful sound that can push through open outdoor spaces. Reception needs pristine vocal clarity for speeches plus dance-floor energy. One single setup rarely fits all — plan modular.

Core Equipment Checklist

For a typical 200–400 guest Indian wedding in Delhi/NCR, the recommended backbone is:

  • Power amplifier: PU8000 (4-channel, high-headroom Class-H) or PU7800 for mid-sized venues. Expect ₹28,000–₹42,000 wholesale in Delhi.
  • DJ mixer with DSP: KMC8 or MG-P32X for larger events with multiple inputs (DJ, mics, band). Budget ₹18,000–₹55,000 depending on channels.
  • Top speakers (mid-high): Pair of 15" 2-way PA speakers, 500–800W RMS each. ₹14,000–₹22,000 per pair.
  • Subwoofers: Dual 18" subs for sangeet/reception. Bass carries the dance floor. ₹16,000–₹28,000 per cabinet.
  • Wireless microphones: Dual UHF handhelds for host/MC plus 1 lapel for pheras. ₹4,500–₹9,500 per set.
  • Cables & accessories: Speakon, XLR, 16A power, DI box, stands. Budget ₹5,000–₹8,000.

Wattage Math — How Loud is Loud Enough?

A simple rule for Indian outdoor weddings: allocate roughly 5–8 watts RMS per guest for clean, non-distorted sound. 300 guests = 1500–2400W total system power. Indoor halls can go a little lower (3–5W per guest) because walls reflect energy. Always oversize — running an amp at 60% is far cleaner than running it at 100%.

Stage + DJ Layout

Keep the DJ console 2–3 meters behind the stage front line so vocals don't bleed into dance music. Use PA systems on tall stands (ear height for seated guests) facing slightly inward to cover the dance floor. Subwoofers go on the ground, centered under the stage — never raise subs, you lose bass coupling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most complaints at Indian weddings come from three mistakes: underpowered amplifiers causing distortion on high-volume moments, wireless mics on same frequency causing drop-outs, and speakers placed behind microphones causing feedback howl. A good DJ mixer with proper gain staging and a notch filter solves 90% of these.
