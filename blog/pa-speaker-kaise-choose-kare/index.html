How to Choose PA Speakers for Your DJ Setup in India

📅 20 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

Picking the right PA speakers decides whether your DJ booth sounds like a warm, full wedding night — or a harsh, tiring blast that clears the floor. PA (Public Address) speakers are built for live events: they throw sound far, stay clear at high volumes, and survive hours of continuous use. At Punishka Trading in Rohini, New Delhi, we help DJs, event companies and tent-house owners pick PA systems every day. Here is how we guide them.

Active vs Passive PA Speakers

Active speakers have the amplifier built in. You just plug in the signal from your mixer and they play. They are simpler to deploy and perfect for small DJs and mobile setups. Passive speakers need an external amplifier like the PU8000 or PU7800. They are cheaper per watt, easier to repair, and scale better — every serious wedding and concert operator in Delhi runs passive for this reason.

Driver Size — What the Inches Mean

  • 10-inch drivers: Good for vocals, small halls up to 60 people, near-field monitors.
  • 12-inch drivers: The most popular all-rounder. Handles vocals + music for 100–250 people.
  • 15-inch drivers: Industry standard for Indian weddings, sangeets and live bands. 250–600 people.
  • 18-inch drivers: Dedicated subwoofers — never for vocals. Used for low-end bass in large events.

Wattage and Coverage

For weddings in Delhi NCR, a common pairing is two 15-inch tops at 600W RMS each plus two 18-inch subs at 800W RMS each. That covers roughly 500 guests with headroom. Cross-check the speaker's SPL (sound pressure level) — look for 125 dB peak or higher for outdoor use.

Horn coverage matters too. A 90° × 60° horn throws sound farther in a narrow space (banquet hall), while a 90° × 90° horn spreads wider (open lawn). We stock both in our PA systems range.

Cabinet Material and Build

Indian PA speakers come in three cabinet grades: plywood (best durability and sound), MDF (mid-level, budget choice) and molded plastic (lightest, for mobile DJs). For permanent installations and tent houses, always go plywood — it survives monsoon, transport jolts and years of use.

Matching Speakers to Amplifier

Your amplifier should deliver 1.5x the speaker's RMS. A 600W RMS speaker wants an 800–1,200W amp channel. Run a KMC8 DSP in between for crossover, limiter and EQ — this is the single biggest upgrade for sound quality and speaker safety.

सही PA Speaker कैसे चुनें?

PA Speaker तय करते हैं कि आपकी DJ night गर्म, भरी-पूरी sound देगी या cheap और harsh लगेगी। PA यानी Public Address — ये speakers बड़े events के लिए बनाए जाते हैं और घंटों बिना रुके clear sound देते हैं।

Active बनाम Passive

Active speakers में amplifier अंदर लगा होता है — mixer से सीधे जुड़ कर बजते हैं। छोटे mobile DJs के लिए बढ़िया। Passive speakers को बाहरी amplifier जैसे PU8000 चाहिए — per watt सस्ते, repair आसान और scale करने में बेहतर। दिल्ली के हर professional wedding DJ का first choice passive ही है।

Driver Size का मतलब

  • 10 इंच: Vocals और 60 लोगों तक छोटे hall
  • 12 इंच: सबसे popular all-rounder — 100–250 लोग
  • 15 इंच: Indian शादियों का standard — 250–600 लोग
  • 18 इंच: सिर्फ Subwoofer के लिए — कभी vocals नहीं

Wattage और Coverage

दिल्ली NCR की शादियों के लिए आम combo है — 2 × 15" tops (600W RMS) + 2 × 18" subs (800W RMS)। लगभग 500 मेहमानों को आराम से cover करता है। Speaker की SPL 125 dB peak या ऊपर देखें outdoor के लिए।

Cabinet Material

तीन grade होते हैं — Plywood (सबसे टिकाऊ और best sound), MDF (मध्यम budget) और Plastic (हल्का, mobile DJs के लिए)। Tent-house और permanent setup में हमेशा plywood लें — बारिश, transport और सालों का use झेलता है।

Amplifier से Match कैसे करें?

Amplifier हमेशा speaker के RMS का 1.5 गुना — 600W RMS speaker के लिए 800–1200W amp। बीच में KMC8 DSP लगाएं — crossover, limiter और EQ के लिए। यह sound quality और speaker safety दोनों बढ़ाता है।
