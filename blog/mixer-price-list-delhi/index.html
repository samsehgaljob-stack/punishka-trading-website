Analog Mixers — The Workhorses

Analog mixers still dominate small-to-medium DJ setups in India because they are cheap, rugged, and repairable by any local technician. At ₹4,500–8,000 you get a 6-channel mixer good for home parties. At ₹15,000 level you step up to metal chassis, phantom power for condenser mics, and Bluetooth input. For rental-grade durability, always check the input PCB thickness and stepped-gain pots.

Digital DSP Mixers — Why They're Worth It

The KMC8 in the ₹28–36K range is the most popular digital mixer we ship from Delhi. It has onboard DSP, 8 XLR inputs, 99 reverb presets, touchscreen control, and recordable effects chains. For weddings with multiple mic performers and a live singer, digital gives you instant recall of scenes — impossible on analog. See our full mixer catalog for specs.

MG-P32X — Our Most Popular 32-Channel

For ₹1.25–1.55 lakh wholesale, the MG-P32X delivers 32 channels, motorized faders on flagship variants, built-in effects engine, and dual Cat6 stagebox output. It's become the standard choice for Delhi NCR event companies running 1000+ guest shaadi setups.

Price-Influencing Factors

Mixer prices fluctuate with USD exchange rates, import cycles, and GST slab changes. Prices we quote here are indicative — for locked-in quotes, WhatsApp us with your exact channel count and feature list. We also offer EMI on mixers above ₹30,000.

Buying in Delhi — What to Check

Don't buy a mixer without testing all channels physically. Bring your own XLR cable, plug into every input, run a 1kHz test tone, and check for clean level meters with no crackle. Any genuine PA system supplier in Delhi will encourage live testing — if they refuse, walk away.
