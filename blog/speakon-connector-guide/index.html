What is a Speakon Connector?

A Speakon connector (originally developed by Neutrik) is the professional standard connector for connecting amplifiers to speakers in DJ and PA systems. If you have ever seen a thick cable with a round, twist-lock connector going from an amp rack to a speaker cabinet, that is a Speakon connection. They are the safest, most reliable, and most durable method of making speaker-level connections — far superior to the banana plugs and bare wire terminals still common in lower-budget Indian setups.

Types of Speakon Connectors

NL2 — 2-Pole Speakon

The most common type. Has two contacts (1+ and 1−) for a single speaker channel. Standard for most DJ cabinet connections — connecting one amplifier output to one speaker. Available in Delhi for ₹80–₹250 per plug depending on quality (import vs local).

NL4 — 4-Pole Speakon

Has four contacts (1+, 1−, 2+, 2−). This allows one cable to carry two independent speaker channels, or it can be wired for bi-amp configurations where separate amplifier channels power different drivers in the same cabinet. Very common in professional rigs. Price: ₹120–₹350 per plug.

NL8 — 8-Pole Speakon

Used in complex installations with multiple amplifier channels running through a single multicore cable. Rare in typical DJ setups but found in large venue installations and broadcast rigs.

Why Speakon vs Banana Plug vs Bare Wire?

  • Safety: Speakon contacts are fully insulated when the connector is not inserted — no risk of accidental short circuits or electric shock. Banana plugs and bare wire terminals carry live voltage at the tips.
  • Security: Speakon connectors twist and lock in place — they cannot accidentally pull out during a performance. Banana plugs can be accidentally kicked loose.
  • Contact quality: High-current silver-plated contacts in Speakon connectors ensure low resistance for clean, high-power signal transfer
  • Durability: Speakon connectors are rated for thousands of connect/disconnect cycles. Banana plugs wear out and lose contact quality much faster.
  • Professionalism: All professional amplifiers and speakers (including PU8000, PU7800) are fitted with Speakon output jacks as standard

How to Wire a Speakon Connector

  1. Strip the outer jacket of your speaker cable to expose the two conductors
  2. Strip 8–10mm of insulation from each conductor
  3. Unscrew the Speakon housing and slide the cable through the strain relief
  4. Connect the positive conductor to the 1+ terminal
  5. Connect the negative conductor to the 1− terminal
  6. Tighten all screws firmly, reassemble the housing, and tighten the strain relief
  7. Test with a multimeter before connecting to equipment

Speakon Cable for Indian DJ Use

For professional DJ use, always use at least 2.5mm² (13 AWG) speaker cable inside your Speakon cables. For high-power connections (1000W+), use 4mm² (11 AWG) cable to minimise power loss over long cable runs.
