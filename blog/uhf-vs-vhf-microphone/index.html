UHF vs VHF Microphone — Which is Better for DJ?

📅 20 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

Wireless microphones are the lifeblood of every wedding DJ, anchor and live performer in India. When you shop for one, you will always face the same question: UHF or VHF? Both are real technologies, both work, but one is clearly better in 2025. At Punishka Trading, Rohini, New Delhi, we sell hundreds of wireless mics every month — here is our honest verdict.

What is VHF?

VHF (Very High Frequency) wireless mics work in the 169–217 MHz band. They were the first affordable wireless systems in India. Signal travels well through walls and is easy to design cheaply. That is why you still see old VHF mics in schools and temples. Range: typically 30–50 meters indoors.

What is UHF?

UHF (Ultra High Frequency) wireless mics operate in 470–960 MHz. The higher frequency means smaller antennas, less interference, and more available channels. Modern UHF systems offer 60–100+ meter range and can run dozens of mics in the same venue without clash.

Why UHF Wins for DJs

  • Fewer dropouts: UHF has more channels to hop between, avoiding the crowded VHF band that includes FM radio, walkie-talkies and wireless CCTV in Indian cities.
  • Better range: 70m typical UHF vs 40m typical VHF, indoors.
  • Smaller, lighter handhelds: UHF antennas are shorter, making mic bodies comfortable for all-night anchoring.
  • Multi-mic support: UHF systems like our popular dual-mic kits let you run 2–8 mics simultaneously with zero interference, perfect for sangeet with anchor, DJ and performers.
  • Regulatory clarity: India's WPC assigns cleaner UHF bands to consumer wireless mics in 2025.

When VHF Still Makes Sense

Only in three cases: extreme budget under ₹3,500 per mic, single-mic use in a small closed room, or legacy venues with existing VHF installs. For every other case, UHF is the right answer.

Interference Problems in Indian Cities

Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore and Chennai have heavy RF pollution — cellular, WiFi, DTH backhaul, intercoms. VHF suffers the most because its channels overlap with TV broadcast remnants and long-range walkie-talkies. Weddings in banquet halls often report "crackling" or "cutting" mic audio from older VHF units. Switching to UHF solves 90% of these complaints.

Battery Life and Build

Good UHF handhelds run 6–10 hours on 2x AA batteries. Look for metal bodies, pop filters, and on/off switches with lock feature — critical when the bride is handing the mic to the anchor on stage. Our top-selling UHF dual-mic kits ship with proper carry case, spare antennas and 1-year warranty.
