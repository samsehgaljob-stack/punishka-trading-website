UHF vs VHF Microphone — Which is Better for DJ?

📅 20 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

Every wireless microphone sold in India falls into one of two camps: VHF (Very High Frequency) or UHF (Ultra High Frequency). A cheap anchor mic might be VHF at 174–216 MHz, while a professional wedding dual mic is almost always UHF at 500–900 MHz. The difference affects sound quality, interference, range, and price. Here's the Delhi-market guide for 2025.

The Basic Definition

VHF transmits audio on radio frequencies between 30 MHz and 300 MHz. Consumer wireless mics typically use 174–216 MHz. UHF uses frequencies from 300 MHz to 3 GHz — wireless mics commonly sit in 470–960 MHz depending on country regulations.

In India, TRAI has reserved certain UHF bands (specifically 865–868 MHz) as license-exempt for wireless mics, which is why most branded Indian dual wireless mics operate there.

Why UHF Wins for DJ & Wedding Use

  • Less interference: VHF overlaps with FM radio, cordless phones, and taxi radios. UHF is cleaner, especially in crowded weddings with multiple wireless devices.
  • More channels: UHF mics can pick from dozens of sub-frequencies to avoid clashing. VHF has fewer options.
  • Smaller antennas: UHF uses shorter antennas, making mics and receivers more compact.
  • Better audio bandwidth: UHF transmitters can carry wider frequency response, making vocals sound more natural.
  • Diversity reception: Pro UHF receivers have two antennas and pick whichever has the best signal — this virtually eliminates dropouts on dance floors.

Where VHF Still Makes Sense

  • Budget anchor mics for seminars or puja events (₹800–2,000)
  • Fixed installations where frequency is pre-tested and stable
  • Very small rooms with no competing wireless gear

Range & Power

A quality UHF dual wireless mic in India provides 50–100 meter clear range outdoors and 30–50 meters indoors. VHF mics at the same price point typically manage 20–40 meters outdoors and struggle indoors past 15 meters due to wall absorption. For baraat processions, UHF is essentially mandatory.

Battery Life

Modern UHF mics use 2× AA batteries and deliver 8–12 hours of continuous use per transmitter. Premium rechargeable-Li units go 14+ hours. VHF systems vary — older designs can drain batteries in 5–6 hours.

  • Entry UHF single mic: ₹2,500–4,500

  • Broadcast-grade UHF lavalier + handheld combo: ₹35,000–75,000

Setup Tips for Indian Weddings

Always bring 4 spare AA batteries per mic. Mount the receiver's antennas at chest height or higher — floor-level antennas cause dropouts. Keep the receiver within 30 meters of the performance zone.
