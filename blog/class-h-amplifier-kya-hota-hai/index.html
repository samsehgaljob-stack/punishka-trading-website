What is a Class-H Amplifier? Complete Guide for DJs

If you've been shopping for a DJ amplifier in India, you've probably seen the term "Class-H" on product labels. But what does it actually mean? This guide breaks down Class-H amplifier technology in plain language so you can make a smarter buying decision.

Amplifier Classes Explained

Amplifiers are categorized by how their output stage operates. The most common classes in DJ equipment are:

  • Class A: Highest sound quality but very inefficient — runs very hot
  • Class AB: Good sound, moderate efficiency — industry standard for decades
  • Class D: Very high efficiency, compact size — used in powered speakers
  • Class H: Enhanced Class AB with dynamic power rail — best of both worlds

How Does Class-H Technology Work?

A Class-H amplifier uses a standard Class AB output stage but adds a clever trick: it dynamically adjusts the power supply voltage based on the incoming audio signal. When the music is quiet, the amp runs on a lower voltage rail, consuming less power and generating less heat. When the beat drops and the signal peaks, the amplifier instantly switches to a higher voltage rail to deliver full power without distortion.

This means a Class-H amplifier is typically 15–25% more efficient than a comparable Class AB unit. For a DJ running a 2000W system for 6–8 hours at a Delhi wedding, that efficiency translates into lower electricity bills, a cooler-running amplifier, and longer equipment life.

Class-H vs Class D — Which is Better for DJ?

Class D amplifiers (also called "switching" or "digital" amplifiers) are even more efficient than Class-H, often reaching 90%+ efficiency. They are extremely compact and light. However, many professional audio engineers prefer Class-H for large PA setups because Class-H delivers better low-frequency control, tighter bass response, and more natural dynamics — critical for high-volume dance music at Indian weddings and concerts.

If you're pairing your amp with large PA speakers and 18-inch subwoofers, Class-H is the preferred choice among professional DJs in Delhi NCR.

Class-H Amplifier क्या होता है? — पूरी जानकारी

अगर आप DJ amplifier खरीदने की सोच रहे हैं और "Class-H" का नाम सुना है, तो यह guide आपके लिए है। Class-H amplifier technology को आसान भाषा में समझते हैं।

Amplifier Classes क्या होती हैं?

  • Class AB: पुराना standard — ठीक ठाक efficiency, थोड़ा गर्म होता है
  • Class D: बहुत efficient, compact — powered speakers में use होता है
  • Class H: Class AB का upgraded version — ज़्यादा efficient, कम गर्म

Class-H कैसे काम करता है?

Class-H amplifier में एक smart power supply होती है जो music signal के हिसाब से voltage automatically adjust करती है। जब music soft होती है — कम voltage, कम power consumption। जब bass drop होता है — full voltage, full power। इससे amplifier 15–25% ज़्यादा efficient होता है और कम heat generate करता है।

Delhi की शादियों में 6–8 घंटे तक DJ बजाना पड़ता है। Class-H amplifier इस दौरान ठंडा रहता है, बिजली कम खाता है और लंबे समय तक चलता है।

Class-H vs Class D — DJ के लिए कौन बेहतर?

Class D amplifier ज़्यादा efficient होता है और हल्का भी होता है। लेकिन professional DJs बड़े PA setups और 18-inch subwoofers के साथ Class-H prefer करते हैं — क्योंकि Class-H में bass tighter होता है और sound dynamics ज़्यादा natural होते हैं।
