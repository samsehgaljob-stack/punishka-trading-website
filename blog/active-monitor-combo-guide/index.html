Active Monitor + Wired Mic Combo — The Complete Event Setup

An active monitor and wired microphone combo is one of the most practical, reliable, and affordable setups for small to medium events in India. Whether you are running a birthday party, a corporate seminar, a small wedding function, or a school event, this combination gives you everything you need in one compact package — no separate amplifier required.

What Is an Active Monitor?

An active monitor (also called a powered speaker) has a built-in amplifier inside the cabinet. You simply plug in your audio source, connect a microphone, and you are ready to go. Active monitors from Punishka Trading typically feature a 12-inch or 15-inch woofer with a built-in 300W–800W amplifier, balanced XLR inputs, volume control, and sometimes built-in EQ and effects.

Why Use a Wired Mic with Active Monitor?

While wireless mics are popular, a good wired dynamic microphone paired with your active monitor offers several advantages for events:

  • Zero interference risk — no frequency conflicts, no dropouts
  • No batteries needed — always ready, never fails mid-event
  • Better sound in the low-mid range — wired dynamic mics capture warm, full vocals
  • Lower cost — excellent wired mics available from Rs.800 to Rs.3,500

Ideal Combo Setup for Small Events

  • 1x Active 15-inch powered monitor (300W–500W built-in amp)
  • 1x Dynamic wired microphone (XLR)
  • 1x XLR cable (6–10 metres)
  • 1x Basic 4-6 channel passive mixer (optional, for multiple sources)

This setup covers a room of up to 100–150 people cleanly. For outdoor use or larger rooms, add a second active monitor for stereo coverage.

Connecting the Combo

  1. Place active monitor at head height or on a stand, angled toward the audience
  2. Connect wired mic to XLR input on the monitor (or through a small mixer)
  3. Set volume on the monitor to 60–70% to start
  4. Speak into the mic from 10–15cm distance and adjust volume for the room
  5. Avoid pointing the mic at the monitor — keep the mic behind the speaker

Best Use Cases in India

  • Kitty party and mehendi functions
  • School and college cultural events
  • Corporate presentations and seminars
  • Small mandap and temple events
  • DJ backup monitor
