How Dynamic Microphones Work

A dynamic microphone uses electromagnetic induction — a diaphragm attached to a coil moves within a magnetic field to generate an electrical signal. Dynamic mics are:

  • Rugged and durable — can take drops, humidity, and rough handling
  • No phantom power needed — works directly with any DJ mixer
  • Handles high SPL well — won't distort when someone shouts into it
  • Less sensitive — requires the speaker to be close to the mic
  • Feedback resistant — better for open-air events with monitors nearby

Examples: Shure SM58 (the industry standard), any standard wired or wireless handheld DJ mic.

How Condenser Microphones Work

A condenser microphone uses a capacitor (condenser) to convert sound to electricity. Condenser mics are:

  • Highly sensitive — picks up very subtle sounds and nuances
  • Requires 48V phantom power from the mixer
  • Wider frequency response — captures more detail, especially in highs
  • More fragile — sensitive to humidity, heat, and rough handling
  • More prone to feedback — requires careful placement away from monitors

Examples: Studio vocal mics, choir mics, overhead recording mics, presentation microphones on podiums.

Which is Right for Indian DJ & Live Sound Events?

Choose Dynamic Mic For:

  • Wedding anchor/emcee (outdoor or indoor)
  • DJ announcements and shout-outs
  • Live singing at events (vocal performance on stage)
  • Situations with loud stage monitors or feedback risk
  • When the mic will be handled by multiple people/guests

Choose Condenser Mic For:

  • Conference rooms and corporate presentations
  • Choir or instrumental recording
  • Broadcast and streaming setups
  • Controlled studio environments
  • Podium or lectern-mounted mics where feedback is managed

What About Wireless Condenser Mics?

Wireless condenser systems (like lapel/lavalier mics) are ideal for corporate events, theatre performances, and fitness instructors. However, they are more expensive (₹8,000–₹30,000) and require careful gain structure to avoid feedback. For standard DJ events, stick with wireless dynamic.
