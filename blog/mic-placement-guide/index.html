Why Microphone Placement Is Critical at Live Events

Even the best wireless microphone will sound poor if placed incorrectly. At live events in India — from weddings and corporate functions to concerts and religious gatherings — proper microphone placement is the difference between crystal-clear vocals and a feedback-filled disaster. This guide covers everything you need to know about positioning microphones for any live event scenario.

The Golden Rule — Keep Mics Behind Speakers

The single most important rule of mic placement is to never position a microphone in front of your PA speakers. Sound from the speakers enters the microphone, gets amplified again, and creates the notorious squeal known as feedback. Always ensure your PA tops are in front of the microphone position and angled away from the mic. This is especially important at small indoor venues in Delhi where speaker placement is limited.

Vocal Mic Distance — The Right Technique

For handheld vocal microphones, the optimal distance from the mouth is 5–15 cm (2–6 inches). Closer gives more bass (proximity effect) but risks pops and overloading. Farther away gives a thinner, more ambient sound with more room noise. For events, train your performers to keep the mic close for best intelligibility. Lapel/Lavalier Microphone Placement

Lapel mics are ideal for corporate events, anchors, and presenters who need hands-free operation. Best placement is:

  • Clip to collar, tie, or shirt — approximately 20–25 cm below the chin
  • Centre chest gives the most natural sound
  • Avoid placing near jewellery or clothing that rustles
  • Route the cable inside the clothing to avoid handling noise

Wireless Mic Antenna Positioning

For wireless microphone systems, antenna placement is often overlooked. The receiver antennas should:

  • Be positioned with a clear line of sight to the performance area
  • Be elevated — at least at head height or higher
  • Never be placed behind metal surfaces or inside metal rack drawers
  • Be separated from each other by at least 30 cm to avoid interference

Stage Microphone Positioning for Large Events

For stage events with multiple performers, use directional microphones aimed at the sound source and away from floor monitors. Keep microphones away from drum kits, bass amplifiers, and speaker stacks on stage to avoid bleed. Punishka Trading stocks 4-channel and dual wireless mic systems ideal for multi-performer events.

Dealing with Feedback at Events

If you get feedback during an event: immediately reduce the gain on the affected channel, check mic position, and consider using a parametric EQ to notch out the feedback frequency. Building a properly tuned sound system with the right speaker placement is the best long-term solution.
