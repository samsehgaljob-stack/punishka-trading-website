Dual Wireless Mic for Events — Complete Guide

📅 21 April 2026
Englishहिंदी

A dual wireless mic setup is the bare minimum for any serious event in India — weddings with an MC and singer, panels with two speakers, religious events with a reader and a vocalist. This 2025 guide covers what to buy, how to set up frequencies, and how much it costs in Delhi wholesale.

Why Dual Beats Single — Every Time

A single wireless mic means awkward hand-offs between the MC and performer. Worse, if one mic dies mid-event, you have no backup. A dual system gives you redundancy, clean MC-to-performer transitions, and opens up karaoke-style duet possibilities. For ₹5,000–₹9,000 extra over a single mic, the upgrade pays for itself at the first event.

UHF vs VHF — Pick UHF

VHF (Very High Frequency, 170–220 MHz) is cheaper but interferes with TV broadcasts and has shorter range. UHF (Ultra High Frequency, 500–900 MHz) is the professional standard — cleaner signal, more available channels, longer range (up to 100 m line-of-sight). In India, UHF is legal and recommended for events.

Frequency Planning — Avoid Drop-outs

The most common wireless mic failure at Indian weddings is two mics set to the same frequency causing a howl and dropout. A dual system gives you 2 pre-paired channels, but if you're running multiple dual systems together (say 4 or 6 mics total), you must plan channel spacing carefully. Check the manual for recommended frequency chart.

Types of Wireless Mics

  • Handheld dual: Most versatile — MC, vocals, host speeches. Our most-sold category.
  • Lapel (lavalier) dual: For interviews, pheras, corporate panels — hands-free.
  • Handheld + headset combo: Performer uses headset, host uses handheld — great for fitness and dance events.
  • Gooseneck dual: For panel discussions on a podium.

What to Pair With Your Mics

A wireless mic is only as good as the DJ mixer you plug it into. Look for a mixer with phantom power (if you ever upgrade to condenser mics), low-cut filters, and per-channel EQ. The KMC8 or MG-P32X both handle dual wireless perfectly. Pair with a PU8000 for clean vocal amplification with no hiss.

Feedback — How to Kill It

Feedback = mic picks up sound from speaker, loops back. Three fixes: (1) keep mics behind speakers, not in front, (2) use the low-cut filter on your mixer, (3) apply a narrow notch EQ at the howl frequency (usually 2–4 kHz). A good mixer with a 31-band EQ handles this automatically.

Battery Life & Backup

AA battery mics run 6–8 hours per pair. For a full wedding day, carry at least 2 spare AA sets per mic. Rechargeable lithium mics save cost long-term but need planning — always come with a charged backup.
